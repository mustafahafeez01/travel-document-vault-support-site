Sound Familiar?

  • At the gate

    You're at the immigration counter. The officer asks for your visa. You're scrolling through 4,000 photos, heart pounding, while the queue builds behind you.

  • The 6-month trap

    Your passport says 2027. Plenty of time, right? Except most countries reject passports expiring within 6 months of travel. That 2027 passport may already be invalid for your summer trip.

  • Renewal chaos

    A renewal notice arrives. You have no idea when you last renewed, what documents you need, or whether the kids' passports expire at the same time.

  • Scattered everywhere

    Passport photo in your camera roll. Insurance PDF in Google Drive. Licence in an old email. Kids' vaccination cards in Notes. Every document in a different place. Some out of date. Some missing entirely.

What You're Probably Using Now

These are all reasonable choices. Each one works - up to a point.

Camera Roll / Photos

Works: Always with you.

Falls short: Buried in thousands of photos with no expiry tracking, no organization, and no security beyond your phone lock.

Cloud Storage

Works: Has offline mode and syncs across devices.

Falls short: You store your passport, check the expiry date, switch to a calendar app to set a reminder, then remember which folder had your spouse's documents. It's three apps doing the job of one—and none of them know it's a passport that expires.

Password Managers

Works: Great for passwords.

Falls short: Not designed for document images, expiry tracking, or family organization. A passport photo in a password field is a workaround, not a solution.

Note-Taking Apps

Works: Flexible and familiar.

Falls short: No expiry reminders, no guided capture, and your passport photo sits next to your grocery list with no encryption.

Paper Folders

Works: Reliable at home.

Falls short: Useless at an airport in another country. And paper doesn't send you a reminder six months before expiry.

"I'll Remember"

Works: Sometimes.

Falls short: Until it doesn't. And it usually fails the night before a flight, when it's too late to fix anything.

"I used most of these myself. The camera roll. The shared drive. The 'I'll just remember' approach. Here's what I kept wishing existed."

What I Built Instead

Five things that make Travel Document Vault different from everything else.

Your Phone. Your Documents. Nobody Else.

I could have built cloud sync. Every other app does. But I kept coming back to the same question: would I trust a stranger's server with photos of my kids' passports? Travel Document Vault stores everything on your device with AES-256 encryption. No servers. No accounts. No one between you and your family's documents. This isn't a privacy "mode" you toggle on. It's the foundation the entire app is built on.

I made a deliberate choice: no cloud, no analytics, no tracking SDKs, no ads, no accounts. Your documents are encrypted with AES-256 and protected by the Secure Enclave on your device. I can't see your data. I don't want to. Read our full privacy policy.

Expiry Reminders, Months Ahead

Most passport renewals take 6-8 weeks. I built reminders that start 6 months early and cascade down: 3 months, 6 weeks, 1 month, 2 weeks, 1 week, 2 days, 1 day, and even post-expiry follow-ups. Point your camera at any document and OCR detects the expiry date automatically - no typing, no mistakes.

Works for passports, visas, driver's licenses, travel insurance policies, vaccination cards, health insurance - anything you want to be reminded about before it expires.

Family Passport Organizer, One Tap Away

One profile per family member. One place for everyone's documents. Built for the person who carries the mental load - the one who remembers which kid's passport expires first and whether the insurance covers the whole family.

Works Offline. Everywhere.

Spotty airport Wi-Fi? Foreign embassy with no signal? Airplane mode? Your documents are always on your phone, always accessible. No internet required, ever.

One Price. No Subscriptions. Ever.

Pro costs $9.99. Once. Not per month, not per year. Most document apps charge $4-10 monthly - that's $48-120 per year. I charge less than a single month of what others charge annually. Free tier gives you 1 profile, 5 documents, OCR, reminders, and PIN lock.

Made by a parent who needed this. Privacy-first by design.

Quick Answers

What happens if I lose my phone?
Your documents live on your device, so a regular phone backup (iCloud or Google Backup) includes them. When you restore to a new phone, your vault comes with it. Pro users can also create encrypted ZIP backups anytime.
Does it work without internet?
Completely. The app stores everything on your device and never needs an internet connection. OCR processing, reminders, and document viewing all work offline.
Is my data really safe?
Your documents are encrypted with AES-256 on your device and protected by the Secure Enclave. There are no cloud servers, no accounts, and no way for anyone - including me - to access your data. PIN lock is free; biometric lock (Face ID/Touch ID) is available with Pro.
Is it really a one-time purchase?
Yes. Pro is $9.99 once. No subscription, no recurring charges, no "your trial is ending" emails. The free tier is genuinely free - 1 profile, 5 documents, all features including OCR and reminders.
Can I use it for more than passports?
Yes. It supports passports, visas, IDs, driver's licenses, insurance cards, airline tickets, and any document type you need. Each type has its own guided capture overlay.
