Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Travel Document Vault.

Privacy & Data

All your data is stored exclusively on your device. We don't have cloud servers, user accounts, or any way to access your documents. When you save a document, it stays in your phone's secure storage area.

Features

The app supports Passports, National IDs (front + back), Visas/Residence Permits, and Airline Tickets. You can capture documents using your camera, import from your photo library, or import PDF files.

When you add an expiry date to a document, we schedule a cascade of reminders. For passports, you'll get notified at 6 months, 3 months, 6 weeks, 1 month, 2 weeks, 1 week, and on the expiry day. Different document types have different schedules optimized for their renewal timelines.

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) automatically detects expiry dates from your documents. Point your camera at a passport or visa, and the app will try to read the expiry date. All processing happens on your device — images are never uploaded. You always confirm detected dates before they're saved.

The app uses profiles to organize documents by family member, but all data stays on your device. There's no shared access or sync between devices. If you need to share a document, you can export it using the system share sheet and send it via AirDrop, email, or messaging.

Yes! The app works completely offline. You can add documents, view them, and receive reminders without any internet connection. Perfect for traveling.

Troubleshooting

Check that notifications are enabled for Travel Document Vault in your device Settings → Notifications. Also ensure you've added an expiry date to your document — reminders are only scheduled when there's an expiry date.

OCR works best with good lighting and a flat document. Try adjusting the angle or lighting. If detection fails, you can always enter the expiry date manually. OCR is assistive — it's designed to help, not replace careful data entry.
