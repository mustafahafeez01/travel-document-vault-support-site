## LinkedIn Post

Losing your passport abroad is one of the worst feelings in travel.

You're far from home. You don't speak the language. The document that proves who you are is gone.

Here's what most people don't realise: it's a solvable problem — and the travellers who get through it fastest all have one thing in common.

They have a secure digital copy of their passport.

When you walk into your embassy, the first thing they ask for is your passport number, issue date, place of issue, and expiry date. Without a copy, you're trying to recall these under stress in an unfamiliar country.

With one, you hand it over in seconds. That single step shortens the emergency process more than anything else.

The 7 steps if it happens:
1. Check everywhere — jacket, every bag pocket, hotel safe
2. File a police report immediately — you need it for your embassy AND your insurer
3. Call your embassy before you go — most require appointments for emergency passports
4. Apply for an Emergency Travel Document — typically issued within 24–48 hours
5. Notify your travel insurer — keep every receipt from this moment on
6. Check your visa situation — a visa is tied to the lost passport, not you
7. Get home, then replace the passport properly

The preparation is a 5-minute job. The fallout from not doing it can be days.
