## LinkedIn Post

You've booked flights for a trip five months from now.

You pull out everyone's passports to double-check.

One expires in five months and three weeks.

Now you need to know exactly how long passport renewal takes — because the maths suddenly matters.

Here's what most people don't realise: the published timelines are averages, not guarantees. And during peak season — January through March, and June through August — the real-world wait is often significantly longer.

Current typical timelines:
→ US: 4–6 weeks standard, 2–3 weeks expedited
→ UK: ~10 weeks standard (digital renewal available for eligible UK residents)
→ Australia: 3–6 weeks standard
→ Canada: 20+ business days standard, 10 days urgent

The $60 or £15 expedite fee is the cheapest travel insurance you'll ever buy.

And the real fix? Renew when you hit 12 months. That way you're never doing urgent maths with a booking confirmation open in another tab.
