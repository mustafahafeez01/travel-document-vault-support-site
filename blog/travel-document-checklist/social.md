## LinkedIn Post

Most travel document checklists are just a flat list.

Passport. Visa. Insurance.

They tell you what to bring — not when to check. And that's exactly where people get caught out.

The family who realise at check-in that one passport expires in five months.
The solo traveller who booked without checking their visa requirement.
The business traveller who forgot which passport their corporate visa is tied to.

The checklist that actually works is organised by when you need to act.

At booking (before you pay):
→ Check every passport expiry against the destination's validity requirement — not just whether it's "valid"
→ Check visa requirements for every person travelling
→ Check if a child consent letter is needed (one parent not travelling? Some countries require it)

Three months before:
→ Last comfortable window to renew a passport on standard processing
→ Last comfortable window to apply for most visas

The night before:
→ Confirmations only. Not discoveries.

At the airport:
→ Passports, boarding passes, insurance certificate, visa docs — kept on you, not in checked luggage.

Different trips need different documents. Family trips need every child checked individually. Business trips need the corporate visa confirmed in the right passport.
