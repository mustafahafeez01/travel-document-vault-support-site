You're at a border crossing. Your phone has no signal. The immigration officer asks to see your visa details.

A passport organizer app with cloud-only storage fails completely. An app that requires an internet connection fails completely. You cannot access your documents when you need them most.

This is the problem most passport apps gloss over in their App Store descriptions. The best ones work fully offline and store your documents on-device, not on some company's server. No account required. No cloud upload. Just your passport, visa, and ID scans encrypted and ready, whether you have internet or not.

For families managing four or five passports, multiple visas, and renewal dates, this approach — offline-first, no-account storage — is the only one that makes genuine sense for sensitive identity documents.

## Instagram Carousel Script

**Slide 1 — Hook**
At a border. No signal. Your passport app won't load.

**Slide 2 — The Problem**
Four passports. Multiple visas. Expiry dates. Travel documents scattered across your phone, email, and photos. One organiser makes it manageable.

**Slide 3 — Data Safety**
Apps that store data on their servers can be breached. Apps that store everything on-device have nothing to breach. Your documents stay with you, not with a company.

**Slide 4 — Offline Matters**
You need your passport details when internet is unreliable: international borders, transit zones, rural travel, after losing your SIM abroad. Your app must work offline.

**Slide 5 — Multi-Person Tracking**
A family of four needs to track four separate expiry dates. Individual reminders for each traveller. Individual document uploads per person. Single-person apps don't work.

**Slide 6 — What to Avoid**
Red flags: requires an account, free with ads, needs internet to view documents, no privacy policy, cloud-only storage, subscription pricing for basic features.
