## LinkedIn Post

It's 11pm the night before a family holiday.

One child's passport is found. One child's is missing. The third is in a drawer somewhere.

Sound familiar?

Family travel document chaos isn't about being disorganised. It's about having no system — so every trip starts from scratch, hunting through the same drawers.

Here's the system that actually works:

**What to keep in one place:**
Every passport, every visa, every travel insurance document — one dedicated travel wallet or file. Not spread across the house.

**The expiry trap most families fall into:**
You renew your own passport, set a reminder, and completely forget your youngest's expires eight months later. No alarm goes off. You book flights a year later. The check-in agent flags it.

Expiry tracking needs to be a system, not a memory exercise. Calendar reminders work if you'll actually maintain them. A spreadsheet works. An app that reminds you automatically works best for families with multiple passports at different stages.

**Digital backups:**
A clear scan of each passport's data page, stored in an encrypted app — not your photo library. If your phone is stolen and someone gets into your camera roll, they have your passport details, date of birth, and face. That's identity fraud territory.

The backup doesn't get you through immigration. But it gets you to the embassy in minutes, not hours, when something goes wrong.

**Children's passports:**
5-year expiry, not 10. They're the ones families most often miss.
