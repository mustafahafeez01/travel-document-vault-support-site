## Instagram Carousel Script

**Slide 1: Hook**
You're 2 AM in a Bangkok hospital. Appendicitis. The surgeon needs your insurance details immediately. Your policy is in an email. You have no internet. What do you do.

**Slide 2: The Problem**
Most travellers buy insurance and never look at it again. When you actually need it—during a medical crisis, theft, or flight cancellation—it's impossible to find. Email requires internet. Your phone might be dead. You're stressed.

**Slide 3: What to Save**
Before you fly: your policy number, 24-hour emergency line, coverage limits, and exclusions. Don't leave these buried in a PDF. Extract them. Screenshot them. Have them accessible without internet.

**Slide 4: Storage Strategy**
Download your insurance PDF to your phone. Screenshot your policy number page. Screenshot emergency contacts. Save both to a folder labelled clearly (e.g. "Insurance 2026"). Also store a copy in cloud with offline access. Keep a physical backup card in your passport.

**Slide 5: The Biggest Mistake**
Most medical claims are rejected because travellers didn't get pre-authorisation before treatment. Before any treatment abroad: call your insurer's emergency line. One phone call. Get a confirmation number. The hospital bills them directly. No call = no reimbursement.

**Slide 6: Key Numbers to Have Accessible**
Policy number. 24-hour emergency line (not the sales number). Your date of birth. Your passport number. Keep these on a card in your passport and screenshots on your phone. That's all you need in a crisis.

## Brand Notes

- No exclamation marks throughout
- British English (travellers, organised, recognised, etc.)
- No AI slop (no "in today's world", "seamlessly", "empower", "look no further")
- Calm, practical tone—scenario-driven, not fear-mongering
- Real consequences (hospital delays, claim rejections) not vague threats
- Strong hook: person in a medical crisis, unable to access insurance—relatable and consequential

**Visual Concept:**
Dark blue background (navy #1F2937 or darker). Gold accent (amber #FBBF24 or similar). Imagery: close-up of hands holding a phone with a policy PDF on screen, or a travel document on a desk with a passport and insurance card. Keep it minimal, professional. Avoid stock photos of stressed people.
