# Social Media Assets — Visa vs Passport

## LinkedIn Post

Your visa is valid. Your passport just expired. Can you still travel.

In many countries, the answer is yes—but most people don't know this. A visa's validity is independent from your passport's validity. You carry both: your old passport with the valid visa stamped inside, plus your new valid passport. Immigration officers check both documents.

Some countries do require you to transfer the visa to your new passport first. That's the catch.

The confusion happens because a visa *appears* to live in your passport. But it's actually a separate permission granted by the destination country, with its own expiry date.

This distinction catches frequent travellers off guard. Know your destination's rules before departure—check the official embassy website for your specific situation.

## Twitter/X Thread

**Tweet 1:**
A passport is your government's official statement of who you are and which country issued it. Every border official you meet will examine it. It's non-negotiable for international travel.

**Tweet 2:**
A visa is something completely different. It's written permission from a destination country saying you're allowed to enter. No valid passport means you can't leave home. No required visa means you won't be let in—even with a perfect passport.

**Tweet 3:**
The critical difference: your passport proves your identity. Your visa proves your destination country consents to your arrival. Some countries don't require visas. But every international trip requires a valid passport.

**Tweet 4:**
Here's what catches travellers: your passport expires, you renew it, but your visa—stamped in the old passport—is still valid. You carry both documents. In most countries, this works. In some, you transfer the visa first. Always verify with your destination's embassy.

**Tweet 5:**
Before booking travel, check these sources: IATA Travel Centre (search by nationality and destination), your destination's official embassy website, or your government's travel advice service. Requirements change. What was visa-free may not be. Don't assume. Verify.

## Instagram Carousel Script

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
You're at the airport. Passport is expired. But your visa won't expire for another 6 months. What happens now.

**Slide 2 (The Fact):**
Your visa and passport have separate validity dates. A valid visa in an expired passport can still work. You carry both documents.

**Slide 3 (The Complication):**
Not every country allows this. Some require you to transfer the visa to your new passport before travel. Check your destination's official embassy website.

**Slide 4 (Passport Definition):**
Passport = official government document proving your citizenship and identity. Issued by your country. Required for all international travel.

**Slide 5 (Visa Definition):**
Visa = permission from a destination country to enter. Depends on your nationality and destination. Some routes visa-free, others require it.

**Slide 6 (Where to Check):**
IATA Travel Centre / Official embassy website / Your government's travel advisory. Check 2-3 months before travel. Requirements change.
