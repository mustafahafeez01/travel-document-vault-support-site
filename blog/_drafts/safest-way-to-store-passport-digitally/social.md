# Social Media Assets — Safest Way to Store Your Passport

## LinkedIn Post

Your Google account gets compromised. Within hours, someone has your passport scan, passport number, and date of birth. They use it to open credit accounts, apply for visas, or assume your identity at a border.

This happened to thousands last year.

Here's the thing: it's not about being paranoid. It's about choosing the right storage tool for sensitive documents.

Most people store passport scans in whatever's convenient — iCloud, Google Photos, WhatsApp. These are designed for photos, not identity documents. They sync automatically, they're encrypted by the company (not by you), and if your account is compromised, everything goes.

There's a better way.

Three options, ranked by security:

**1. Dedicated encrypted app** (most secure)
Stores documents on your phone only. AES-256 encryption. No account. No cloud. No remote breach surface. Trade-off: you can't automatically access it across devices.

**2. iCloud Photos with Advanced Data Protection** (strong)
End-to-end encrypted if you enable it. Syncs across Apple devices. Trade-off: your Apple ID is now the master key. If that account is compromised, everything is compromised.

**3. Google Photos** (avoid for passports)
No end-to-end encryption by default. Google's systems process your images. Historically they've had security incidents. Not designed for identity documents.

For families travelling together, a dedicated encrypted app that stores multiple family members' documents (without uploading to the cloud) offers both security and convenience.

Your passport is as sensitive as the physical document. Store it accordingly.
