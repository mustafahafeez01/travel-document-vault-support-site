## LinkedIn Post

It happens more than you'd think.

You're at the airport, luggage checked in, boarding pass in hand.

The check-in agent looks at your passport. Looks up. Looks at you.

"I'm sorry — this expired three months ago."

There are two versions of this problem. One is fixable with time. One requires you to move fast and know exactly who to call.

Version A — You discovered it at home, trip coming up:

If you have 10+ weeks: apply standard renewal now, don't wait.
6–10 weeks: pay for expedited — standard is too tight to gamble on.
Under 6 weeks: call your passport authority directly. Emergency slots exist that aren't visible on the website. UK: 0300 222 0000. US: 1-877-487-2778.
Under 2 weeks: same-day Premium (UK) or regional passport agency emergency appointment (US). You need proof of imminent travel.

Version B — You discovered it at the airport:

Go to the airline desk immediately. Some waive change fees for genuine document emergencies — ask, and get it in writing if they agree.
Then call your passport authority. Same-day and next-day appointments exist. You need to ask for them.

The real fix: set your renewal reminder at 12 months, not 6. That gives you time to renew on standard processing without stress.

Children's passports expire in 5 years, not 10. They're the ones most often missed.
