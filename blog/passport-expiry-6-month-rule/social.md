## LinkedIn Post

An immigration officer looked at my daughter's passport and paused.

"You know this expires in seven months? Some countries won't let you in with less than six months validity."

We were fine. Barely.

I'd checked the passport before booking. Seven months felt like plenty.

What I'd completely forgotten: the 6-month passport rule doesn't count from when you arrive. It counts from when you leave the destination.

So if you fly home on 1 August, your passport needs to be valid until 1 February the following year — minimum. A passport expiring 30 November? Two months short. Denied boarding.

Here's what most people get wrong:

→ They check whether the passport is "valid" — not whether it clears the 6-month window
→ They assume countries that "usually" let people through will let them through
→ They forget that airlines enforce it with TIMATIC before you even reach the gate

Thailand, Indonesia, Vietnam, India, Kenya — all enforce it. Most of Europe and the US don't.

The fix is simple: renew when you drop below 12 months. You'll always carry a buffer.

I wrote a full breakdown with a country table here — link in first comment.
